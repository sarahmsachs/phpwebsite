#dispix: How Disability Looks to Me

About #dispix

On November 11th, 2015, members of PHP1680: "Pathology to Power" presented a photo exhibition about living with a disability. The exhibition featured photo submissions from students and community members, depicting their personal perspectives on life with a disability. #dispix hopes to raise awareness of disability on campus and to amplify the voices and experiences of people with disabilities.

Arthrogryposis multiplex congenita is weird, and rare, and you can feel like the ugliest kid in the world when you have it. I walked like a chimpanzee, and then I wore braces on my legs and back for years, then I was in and out of surgeries, wheelchairs, crutches, you name it. I never got to be pretty, only "brave". I didn't wear shorts until college - i still kind of dread people seeing my legs. Not everyone, though - this picture was taken last summer, in my girlfriend's car, and in a fit of pride I posted it to every social media account I had. I felt gorgeous. She makes me feel gorgeous, not despite of anything but because of everything I am.

One of my biggest daily challenges is walking and balancing, so I don't fall.  Five years ago, I became paralyzed from the neck down from Transverse Myelitis, a rare neuro-immune disease that attacked my central nervous system.  Daily exercise has helped me to relearn the "art of walking, although not graceful or fast, I can walk again!  I've learned to appreciate "this new normal," an activity many of us take for granted in our daily lives. Not thrilled that I can't safely walk in any stylish heel or flat shoe and have to wear sneakers every day, at least my feet can tolerate Coach sneakers!
